Project Idea

A computer hardware-based webstore that has a linked mobile phone application that allows, PC enthusiasts, server engineer’s and novices alike, to build
their desired systems from both a website as well as on the go from a mobile phone application. The goal of my application is to allow any hardware build to be
undertaken anywhere. This application would facilitate those who are on the job as well as those enthusiasts who are out looking to build a new PC. It includes
functions such as building PC systems, building server systems, showing power requirements for systems builds, build guides for first time builders, etc.

As far as my research has lead me, there are only two PC hardware building applications available
(Google play store, PC Builder - Part Picker & Generate Builds!; Apple Store, BuildCores: PC Parts & Builds). Both these applications are limited by the region
they are developed and locked to. They are also limited to buying their parts from third party online suppliers such as Amazon and Newegg, which the Australian
versions of these sites have very limited functionality and stock for buying PC components. While not an inherently bad feature, having direct supply to a
manufacturer would be far more efficient. My vision of a PC building application would circumvent the issues with regional availability and act as an online store
with direct links to all necessary hardware manufacturers.

The focus is to launch the application for end users to allow them to build what they desire on the go; however, I believe this would be counter-intuitive without
a website to be paired with the application. This would allow server engineers who are either upgrading or building a new system to build a system on their phone and
send it to whoever necessary for approval, reducing project downtime. This function of the application would also allow the sending of a quote to a client in the form
of a parts list and breakdown of the items required.
The application isn’t just focused at the working professional but also at D.I.Y hobbyists and PC enthusiasts. A first time PC builder can use this application to help
them through their build step by step, with both a detailed instruction set and a forum with advice from seasoned professionals. They may not have access to a laptop
or another PC to get help when building their system, but most users will have access to a smartphone, where this application comes in handy. Easy to follow step by
step instructions and video tutorials are easily accessible via the application.
The PC enthusiast can take advantage of this application by building their current PC setup, then upgrading the components of their choice to see if they will be
compatible with their current build. The information that will be displayed for them will include cost to performance upgrade, increase or decrease in required power
draw and compatibility of the hardware components.
Many of the current website-based PC building tools have some of these functions but lack certain other functionalities. It makes building a PC for the first time
quite confusing as there is a lot of information to understand before undertaking a new build, which usually requires going to several different websites to gain the
information necessary. This application is an all-encompassing build guide to help all levels of system integrators to complete their builds from start to finish.
